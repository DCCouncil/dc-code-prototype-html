§ 46–421. Violations; prosecutions.

Whoever fails to comply with §§ 46‑416, 46‑416.01, and 46‑420 shall be imprisoned for not more than 6 months, or fined not more than the amount set forth in § 22‑3571.01, or both. Prosecutions for violations of this section shall be conducted by the Attorney General for the District of Columbia.

Effect of amendments

D.C. Law 15-354 substituted “Attorney General for the District of Columbia” for “Corporation Counsel”.

The 2012 amendment by D.C. Law 19-169 substituted “relating to any person who has, or suspected to have, syphilis” for “relating to any person suffering, or suspected to be suffering from, syphilis.”

The 2014 amendment by D.C. Law 20-148 rewrote the section.

Legislative history of Law 19-169

Law 19-169, the “People First Respectful Language Modernization Amendment Act of 2012,” was introduced in Council and assigned Bill No. 19-189. The Bill was adopted on first and second readings on Mar. 6, 2012, and Apr. 17, 2012, respectively. Signed by the Mayor on May 15, 2012, it was assigned Act No. 19-361 and transmitted to Congress for its review. D.C. Law 19-169 became effective on Sept. 26, 2012.
