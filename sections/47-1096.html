History

(Mar. 11, 2015, D.C. Law 20-229, § 2(b), 62 DCR 276.)

Legislative history of Law 20-229

Law 20-229, the “N Street Village, Inc. Tax and TOPA Exemption Act of 2014,” was introduced in Council and assigned Bill No. 20-690. The Bill was adopted on first and second readings on Nov. 18, 2014, and Dec. 2, 2014, respectively. Signed by the Mayor on Dec. 29, 2014, it was assigned Act No. 20-551 and transmitted to Congress for its review. D.C. Law 20-229 became effective on Mar. 11, 2015.

Editor’s notes

Applicability of D.C. Law 20-229: Section 3 of D.C. Law 20-229 provided (1) that the act shall apply for the tax year in which its fiscal effect has been included in an approved budget and financial plan; (2) that the Chief Financial Officer shall certify the date of the inclusion of the fiscal effect in an approved budget and financial plan, and provide notice to the Budget Director of the Council of the certification; and (3) that the Budget Director shall cause the notice of the certification to be published in the District of Columbia Register and that the date of publication of the notice of the certification shall not affect the applicability of the act.
