Repealed.

History

(Mar. 16, 1978, D.C. Law 2-59, § 3, 24 DCR 5975; Apr. 20, 1999, D.C. Law 12-261, § 1234, 46 DCR 3142.)

Prior Codifications

1981 Ed., § 2-101.

1973 Ed., § 2-942.

Legislative history of Law 12-261

Law 12-261, the “Second Omnibus Regulatory Reform Amendment Act of 1998,” was introduced in Council and assigned Bill No. 12-845, which was referred to the Committee of the Whole. The Bill was adopted on first and second readings on December 1, 1998, and December 15, 1998, respectively. Signed by the Mayor on December 31, 1998, it was assigned Act No. 12-615 and transmitted to both Houses of Congress for its review. D.C. Law 12-261 became effective on April 20, 1999.

Editor’s notes

D.C. Law 12-261, Title I, § 1234 ( 46 DCR 3142), eff. April 20, 1999, provided:

“Sec. 1234. The District of Columbia Public Accounting Act of 1977, effective March 16, 1978 (D.C. Law 2-59; D.C. Code § 2‑101 et seq. § 3‑1501 et seq., 2001 Ed. ), relating to the licensure of public accountants is repealed and authority transferred to the Board of Accountancy established by D.C. Code [§ 47-2853.6 § 47‑2853.06, 2001 Ed.].”
